MOST POPULAR DESTINATION

Explore the fascinating satellites and planets that our users adore visiting, and take your time to choose the ideal getaway destination that's perfect for you.

See More

Saturn's rings are made of ice and rock particles, ranging from tiny grains to large boulders. They are believed to be remnants of moons or comets that were torn apart by Saturn's gravity.

SATURN RINGS

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite, formed about 4.5 billion years ago from debris after a giant impact. It has no atmosphere and experiences extreme temperature variations.

MOON

Europa is one of Jupiter's largest moons, covered in a thick layer of ice. Scientists believe it may have a subsurface ocean, making it a potential candidate for extraterrestrial life.

EYROPA

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and the closest to the Sun. It has extreme temperature swings, from 800°F during the day to -300°F at night, with no atmosphere to retain heat.

MERCURY

Black holes are regions of spacetime where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape. They form from the collapse of massive stars and come in various sizes, from stellar to supermassive.

BLACK HOLE

Titan is Saturn's largest moon and the only moon in the solar system with a substantial atmosphere. It has lakes of liquid methane and ethane, and may have conditions similar to early Earth.

TITAN

Neptune is the eighth planet from the Sun and the farthest known planet in our solar system. It has the strongest winds, reaching up to 1,200 mph, and a deep blue color due to methane in its atmosphere.

NEPTUNE

Mars, known as the Red Planet due to iron oxide on its surface, has the largest volcano and canyon in the solar system. It has polar ice caps and evidence of ancient water flows.

MARS

SPACE FUN FACTS

Amazing Discoveries Await

Dive into some mind-blowing facts about the universe that will expand your cosmic perspective.

🌌

The Universe is Expanding

The universe has been expanding since the Big Bang, and it's accelerating due to dark energy.

🪐

Jupiter is a Mini Solar System

Jupiter has over 80 moons, some larger than planets, making it like a mini solar system.

⭐

Stars Can Sing

Stars oscillate and produce sound waves that can be detected as "star songs" by astronomers.

🌑

Black Holes Aren't Black

Black holes emit radiation called Hawking radiation, so they're not completely black.

🌟

A Light Year is Massive

A light year, the distance light travels in a year, is about 6 trillion miles. That's mind-bogglingly far!

🏖️

More Stars Than Sand

There are more stars in the observable universe than grains of sand on all of Earth's beaches combined.

🔇

Space is Silent

Sound waves need a medium to travel, so space is completely silent. No whooshing rockets or booming explosions.

🌋

Mars Has Giant Volcanoes

Olympus Mons on Mars is the largest volcano in the solar system, standing about 13 miles high.
